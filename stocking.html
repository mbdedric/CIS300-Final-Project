Beginner-Friendly Stocking Ideas

Stocking a tank is more than just “how many fish fit.” You need to consider adult size, temperament, and how much waste each species produces.

The table below lists a few simple community ideas for a 10–20 gallon freshwater tank.

Tank Size Example Stocking Notes
10 gallons 1 betta + 6–8 small peaceful shrimp Heavily planted and lots of hiding spots recommended.
15 gallons 8–10 small schooling fish (like neon tetras) + 4–5 snails Works best with stable temperature and a gentle filter.
20 gallons 8–10 schooling fish + 6–8 bottom dwellers (like small cories) Good beginner community if the tank is fully cycled.

Fish That Are Not Beginner-Friendly

Many pet stores sell fish that do not belong in small tanks, such as common goldfish, “monster” fish, or aggressive species that require special setups. Always research the adult size and minimum tank size before you buy.

Basic Compatibility Tips

  • Do not mix fin nippers with long-finned fish like fancy guppies or bettas.
  • Keep schooling fish in groups of at least 6 to reduce stress.
  • Avoid overstocking; less fish with good care is better than a crowded tank.
