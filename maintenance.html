Weekly Maintenance Checklist

  • Test water for ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, and pH.
  • Perform a 20–30% water change using dechlorinated water.
  • Vacuum the substrate lightly to remove debris.
  • Wipe algae from the glass with an aquarium-safe tool.
  • Check filter flow and rinse media in old tank water if needed.

Monthly Maintenance

Once a month, take a closer look at your equipment and overall setup:

  • Inspect heater and thermometer for accuracy.
  • Check cords, lids, and stands for wear or damage.
  • Gently deep clean decor if it is covered in algae or waste.

Common Problems & Quick Responses

Cloudy Water

Often caused by overfeeding or a bacterial bloom in a new tank. Cut back on food, test water, and keep up with water changes.

Algae Everywhere

Reduce the light period, avoid direct sunlight, and make sure you are not overfeeding. Live plants and algae eaters can help, but water quality comes first.

Fish Gasping at the Surface

Test for ammonia and nitrite immediately. Increase surface agitation, perform an emergency partial water change, and consider contacting a local fish store for help.
